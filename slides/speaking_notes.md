# Speaking Notes (15-20 Minutes)

## Slide 1 - Title
Introduce yourself and the project objective: building a practical fairness-aware hiring pipeline in R.

## Slide 2 - Problem Statement
Explain that hiring automation can unintentionally encode bias and why gender fairness is analyzed.

## Slide 3 - Motivation
Discuss 2026 ethical AI expectations, governance, and impact on trust.

## Slide 4 - Literature Highlights
Summarize key contributions from recent IEEE and foundational fairness studies.

## Slide 5 - Methodology
Walk through DALC/MLOps phases and how each phase supports fairness.

## Slide 6 - Dataset Overview
Describe the dataset features, target, and protected attribute.

## Slide 7 - EDA Findings
Show selection disparities and feature distributions by gender.

## Slide 8 - Baseline Model
Explain why baseline logistic regression is needed for comparison.

## Slide 9 - Mitigation Strategy
Describe reweighting and why underrepresented group-outcome pairs receive higher weights.

## Slide 10 - Model Performance
Compare accuracy, AUC, and F1 across baseline and mitigated models.

## Slide 11 - Fairness Metrics
Interpret parity metrics and highlight bias reduction results.

## Slide 12 - XAI Findings
Discuss feature importance and case-level explainability insights.

## Slide 13 - Error Analysis
Explain confusion matrix, FPR/FNR differences, and subgroup reliability.

## Slide 14 - Live Demo
Describe model training, API startup, and endpoint testing flow.

## Slide 15 - UAE Relevance
Connect fair AI hiring to inclusive workforce and responsible innovation goals.

## Slide 16 - Limitations/Future Work
Mention synthetic data and single protected attribute; propose intersectional extension.

## Slide 17 - Conclusion
Summarize that the pipeline is explainable, reproducible, and deployment-ready.

## Slide 18 - References
Mention IEEE-style references and evidence-backed methodology.

## Slide 19 - Q&A
Invite questions and thank the audience.
